Oily Skin — Clarity-first picks

Focus on lightweight hydration, pore care, and oil regulation without stripping.

5 products

Avoid these for oily skin

Heavy face oils Thick balm cleansers Coconut oil Alcohol-free toners that are still drying Over-cleansing (2× daily max)
💡Skipping moisturiser makes oiliness worse — your skin overproduces sebum to compensate. Use a lightweight gel moisturiser every morning.

Dry Skin — Barrier-first picks

Layer humectants under ceramide-rich creams and avoid anything that strips your lipids.

5 products

Avoid these for dry skin

Foaming / SLS cleansers Strong AHAs daily Alcohol-based toners Hot water on face Skipping SPF in winter
💡Apply your serum and moisturiser on slightly damp skin — it helps trap hydration and dramatically improves absorption.

Combination Skin — Zonal picks

Use lighter textures on the T-zone and richer formulas on cheeks. Spot-treat, don't blanket-treat.

5 products

Avoid these for combination skin

One heavy cream everywhere Alcohol-based serums Over-exfoliating the whole face Skipping the T-zone in your routine
💡Treat your face like two separate zones: apply a lighter gel on T-zone and a cream on cheeks. It takes 30 extra seconds and makes a real difference.

Sensitive Skin — Gentle-only picks

Short ingredient lists, fragrance-free, and always patch-test on your jawline first.

5 products

Avoid these for sensitive skin

Fragrance (parfum) Denatured alcohol Strong retinoids Physical scrubs Essential oils (lavender, peppermint)
💡Introduce one new product at a time and wait 2 weeks before adding another. If your skin reacts, you'll know exactly what caused it.

Acne-Prone Skin — Treatment picks

Target active breakouts and congestion with proven actives while keeping the barrier intact.

5 products

Avoid these for acne-prone skin

Comedogenic oils (coconut, flaxseed) Over-drying spot treatments Scrubbing active breakouts Makeup with bismuth oxychloride Touching / picking
💡Moisturising acne-prone skin is non-negotiable. A stripped, dehydrated barrier makes breakouts worse. Choose a non-comedogenic, lightweight moisturiser.
